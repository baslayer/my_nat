<p>Natalie rushed to fill the silence. “Van asked about it. I couldn’t remember. And when I tried to remember, it just kind of felt… lost.”</p>


<p>Lottie’s voice was quiet. “You were upset.”</p>


<p>Natalie blinked. “I was?”</p>


<p>“We were watching the movie and you started breathing really heavily and when the girls started planning the murder… I stopped the movie. But you couldn’t calm down.”</p>


<p>Natalie swallowed. “I don’t remember that.”</p>


<p>“I know,” Lottie said, calm. “You asked me for help.”</p>


<p>Natalie looked up sharply.</p>


<p>“You were shaking a lot. I thought maybe you were having a panic attack. Then you looked at me and asked me to <em> do the thing </em>.”</p>


<p>“I… did?”</p>


<p>“You were crying,” Lottie said, her tone soft but clinical. “So I helped you. I brought you down. I took the memory and held it for you. That’s why it’s gone.”</p>


<p>Natalie’s throat tightened. “You <em> took </em> it?”</p>


<p>“I protected you.” Lottie corrected her. “It was too much. You asked me to help you so I did.”</p>


<p>Natalie sat with that. The words felt slippery in her mind, impossible to hold.</p>


<p>“Did we finish the movie?”</p>


<p>“No.”</p>


<p>“What did we do after?”</p>


<p>“We went to the library. I wanted to take your mind off of it. I wanted to make you feel better.”</p>


<p>Natalie blinked. That part she remembered. She sat for a moment, considering Lottie’s version of events and weighed it against what she had in her memory.</p>


<p>“Did you ask me, before you did it?” Her voice lacked conviction.</p>


<p>Lottie leaned forward. “You asked me to do it.”</p>


<p>“I asked you to take my memory?”</p>


<p>Lottie was silent a moment before speaking again. “I told you.  You asked me to <em> do the thing </em>. I guess you know what that means.” She looked at her pointedly.</p>


<p>Nat glanced at her and saw the depth in her eyes and knew. The good feeling. <em> I asked for the feeling.  </em></p>


<p>“Do you want the memory back?” Lottie asked.</p>


<p>Natalie hesitated. “I don’t know.”</p>


<p>Lottie nodded. “That’s okay.”</p>


<p>Another beat passed.</p>


<p>“Do you trust me?” she asked.</p>


<p>Natalie finally looked at her. “Yes Lottie.”</p>


<p>Lottie smiled faintly. “Then let me carry that part for you. Until you’re ready.”</p>


<p>Natalie exhaled. “Okay.”</p>


<p>She stood slowly and walked over, sinking to her knees at Lottie’s feet. “Thank you Lottie.”</p>


<p>Lottie touched her hair, gently smoothing it back. “Good girl.”</p>


<p>Natalie closed her eyes.</p>
